February 2, 2020
On Feb. 2, the Philippines saw the first confirmed death from Covid-19 outside mainland China.
The coronavirus death toll has risen to at least 360 people worldwide.
February 5, 2020
On Feb. 5, the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation announced that it will spend up to $100 million to improve detection, isolation and treatment efforts for Covid-19.
The W.H.O. stated that there had been a substantial increase in new cases over the last 24 hours, on a scale not seen since the beginning of the epidemic.
A cruise ship in Japan, the "Diamond Princess", quarantined over 3.600 passengers aboard.
By Mar. 16, 712 out of the 3.711 passengers had tested positive for the virus.
The number of reported cases in Wuhan has exceeded the 10.000 mark.
February 9, 2020
On Feb. 9, just over 40.000 cases were confirmed in mainland China and almost 30 000 cases in Hubei.
The number of coronavirus deaths in China has risen to over 800, surpassing the number of victims from the SARS epidemic of 2002-03, which killed 774 people.
February 15, 2020
On Feb. 15, the first death outside Asia was confirmed, an 80 year old Chinese tourist in France.
At that time there were 12 confirmed cases in France.
February 21, 2020
On Feb. 21, Italy confirmed 17 new cases, bringing the total to 20. The authorities also reported the first death in Italy.
Iran announced 13 new cases, bringing the total up to 18, and South Korea confirmed 100 more cases, bringing the total to 204.
February 23, 2020
On Feb. 23, Italy confirmed 73 new cases, bringing the total up to 155.
Prime minister Giuseppe Conte announces measures which place around 50.000 people in lockdown in an effort to bring the virus under control.
